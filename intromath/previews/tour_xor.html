Using negation, conjunction, and disjunction, construct a statement with the same meaning as “either Alex\mathrm{Alex}Alex is hungry or Alex\mathrm{Alex}Alex is asleep”.

Whenever you encounter an exclusive or, you can prove it by first translating it into the equivalent statement that only uses negation, conjunction, and disjunction.

Alex\mathrm{Alex}Alex is hungry and Alex\mathrm{Alex}Alex is not asleep, or Alex\mathrm{Alex}Alex is not hungry and Alex\mathrm{Alex}Alex is asleep.
